How can a person be a full-time office assistant if they only work at your office virtually? You'd be surprised, though. Someone who works remotely can still perform many of the typical "assistant jobs" seen around the office, such as data entry, bookkeeping, and administrative support.


A virtual office assistant is an employee that works on contractual agreements independently and assists with administrative, creative, or technical work. Typically, this entails performing tasks similar to those of a manager or office secretary from a remote location, using the virtual office assistant's equipment.


For many businesses, hiring an office assistant is the only option: onboarding someone who is physically present in the office for work. But we'll explain why that doesn't have to be the case. A virtual assistant, on the other hand, is a cost-effective, fully productive, and skilled employee who can handle any office job you demand.
